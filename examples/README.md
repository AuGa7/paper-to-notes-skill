# Showcases

These examples show what `paper-to-notes` is meant to produce. They are not full paper reproductions; they are sample outputs that demonstrate the skill's structure, level of detail, and research workflow.

## Example Paper

**Attention Is All You Need**

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762
- NeurIPS proceedings: https://papers.nips.cc/paper/7181-attention-is-all-you-need

The paper is a good showcase because it has a clear architecture, concrete experiments, known reproduction challenges, and enough impact that many readers can judge whether the notes are useful.

## Why These Examples Are Useful

- They show that the skill does more than summarize abstracts.
- They keep author claims separate from interpretation.
- They expose missing details and reproduction risks.
- They produce artifacts a student, engineer, or researcher could act on.
